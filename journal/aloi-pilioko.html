Vanuatu & Fiji

The “Picasso of the Pacific”

Aloï Pilioko in the PacificaRomania collection.

Experience the searing light and lush biodiversity of Oceania through the vibrant works of Aloï Pilioko. Born in Wallis and Futuna and deeply rooted in Vanuatu and Fiji, Pilioko brilliantly bridges ancient Polynesian tradition with modern expressionism.

His featured works transform island life into a universal language of colour. Nautical Rhythms dives into deep azure waters, using bright yellows to symbolise the spiritual mana of a successful sea harvest. Tautahi & Tamahina captures the fiery duality of the totemic rooster, contrasting sacred reds with ethereal whites. Fidji celebrates the “Pacific Way” with a rich jungle palette, floral crowns, and expressive eyes that reflect the watchful presence of the ancestors.

Echoes Across the Collection

The large, expressive eyes in Pilioko’s Fidji reflect a sense of watching ancestors — directly echoing the visual strategy of the pale Asaro spirit mask from Papua New Guinea elsewhere in the collection. Whether rendered in stark, ghostly river clay to invoke fear, or through lush, colourful paint to celebrate heritage, the exaggerated or hollowed eye serves as a vital motif across Melanesia and Polynesia for manifesting an active, observing ancestral presence.

Nautical Rhythms’ dominant azure blue and symbolic yellow is mirrored in the collection’s great Australian water-dreaming painting, which similarly uses networks of blue and white dots punctuated by yellow concentric circles to map life-giving water systems and ancestral energy across the desert. Both artists use rhythmic colour to map the physical and spiritual sustenance of their environments.

Pilioko’s works visually anchor the “Pacific Way” — celebrating communal gathering, natural rhythm, and shared heritage — in a manner that complements the towering mortuary poles of Arnhem Land elsewhere in the collection, which serve as the ultimate physical and spiritual pillar for their communities, a structural bridge between the physical world and the ancestral realm.
