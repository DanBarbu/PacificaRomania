The Collection · Romanian Embassy, Canberra 2019–2020

Over 150 works, from the Western Desert to the cosmic sea

Assembled as a cultural-diplomacy initiative and first exhibited at the Embassy of Romania in Canberra, the collection gathers Aboriginal Australian painting, Melanesian ritual sculpture, Micronesian atoll craft, and Polynesian chiefly art — later joined by a chapter of Island Southeast Asia. Browse by region below.

Part I

Australia

Dreaming, desert law, and the world’s longest living artistic tradition — from Papunya dot painting to Arnhem Land hollow-log coffins.
Part II

Melanesia

The great engine of ritual invention: Sepik masks, Solomon Islands prow guardians, Vanuatu slit gongs, and the Fijian kava ceremony.
Part III

Micronesia

A cartographic metaphysics of the atoll: Nauru’s resilience, Tuvalu’s sinking horizon, and Kiribati’s shark-tooth cosmology.
Part IV

Polynesia

Art as genealogical inscription: Samoan siapo and ‘ava, Tongan ngatu, and the taonga of Aotearoa — tiki, toki, and pounamu.
Later Chapter

Island Southeast Asia

Beyond the Pacific: Dayak baby carriers, Batak medicine vessels, and the shamanic masks of the Malaysian Orang Asli — explored in depth in the Journal.
