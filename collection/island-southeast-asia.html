Island Southeast Asia

Guardians of the Threshold.

Australia Melanesia Micronesia Polynesia Island SE Asia

Beyond the Pacific proper, the collection extends into Island Southeast Asia — Borneo, Sumatra, the Malay Peninsula, and Myanmar — where the same Eliadean grammar of threshold, ancestor, and sacred centre recurs in carved hardwood, buffalo horn, and cast bronze. These objects are explored at length in the Journal; photography is being added as the collection is catalogued.

Borneo, Sumatra & the Malay Peninsula

Guardians of the Threshold

Papot (Baby Carrier)

Dayak, Borneo · Carved wood, rattan, beadwork

A portable sacred centre — a spiritual shield carved with fierce guardian faces, serpentine motifs, and hornbill wings, invoking ancestral spirits to carry an infant safely through both jungle and threshold.

Read the curatorial essay →

Naga Morsarang

Batak, Sumatra · Carved buffalo horn medicine container

A hollow buffalo horn holding ritual medicine (pupuk), animated by a carved stopper of ancestral figures — in its fullest form a complete cosmogram uniting horn, serpent-dragon, and lineage into one operative instrument.

Read the curatorial essay → · Indonesia Museum

Moyang Siamang

Jah Hut or Mah Meri, Peninsular Malaysia · Carved hardwood

A carved ancestor-spirit of the primate siamang, one of more than 460 moyang forms in the animist atlas of the Orang Asli, for whom the gibbon’s dawn call marks the threshold between the human and the more-than-human world.

Read the curatorial essay →

Mah Meri Ancestral Masks

Mah Meri (Senoi), Selangor, Malaysia · Carved wood

Masks that materialise the Moyang, carved by shamans said to be “chosen” through dream — the very rituals Mircea Eliade drew on in Shamanism to describe the shaman as psychopomp.

Myanmar

The Sacralization of Measure

Chinthe Master Weight

Burma, 18th–19th century · Bronze

A lion-gryphon “master weight,” sentinel of the marketplace axis mundi and guardian of the pagoda, representing the solar and fire element in a merchant’s set of scales that quietly unites Heaven, Earth, and Underworld.

Read the curatorial essay →

Elephant & Fish Weights

Burma, 18th–19th century · Bronze

Elephant weights ground the set in the Earth element; rarer fish and dolphin weights evoke the primordial waters — together with the Chinthe, a complete cosmology cast in bronze for daily trade.
