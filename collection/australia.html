Australia

Dreaming, Desert Law & the Longest Living Tradition.

AustraliaMelanesiaMicronesiaPolynesiaIsland SE Asia

The emergence of contemporary Aboriginal painting at Papunya in 1971 continued the world’s longest unbroken artistic tradition. These canvases are at once narrative atlases, ceremonial maps, and legal documents — the dot technique a “spiritual privacy filter” that shields restricted knowledge while preserving ancestral form. Alongside the desert painters sit the Hermannsburg watercolourists and the Yolŋu hollow-log coffins of Arnhem Land.
